The Field

How Ultraviolet compares to everything else putting assets on Bitcoin.

The strongest incumbent

Taproot Assets

Lightning Labs' protocol is the most shipped competitor: v0.8.0, with USDT live on Lightning since March 2026, static reusable addresses, multi-path payments, and RFQ edge liquidity. It is also a cousin — client-side validated, asset data off-chain, Bitcoin as the anchor. The divergence is three architectural commitments:

OWNERSHIP

The UTXO is the asset

A Taproot Asset lives inside a taproot output — ownership is secp256k1 Schnorr. Elegant (commitments hide in ordinary-looking spends) but: a UTXO per holder, an on-chain spend per transfer, and total quantum exposure with no PQ roadmap. Ultraviolet: hash-based keys in client-side data, keyless 64-byte records, receive with an empty wallet — a quantum thief who steals the anchor UTXO steals nothing.

VALIDATION

Proofs grow forever

Provenance proofs grow O(lineage) — every recipient revalidates the custody chain, and proofs fatten each hop, heaviest exactly where adoption succeeds. They also reveal history and amounts to every recipient; no ZK layer exists or is planned. Ultraviolet: one 1,242 KB recursive STARK, constant across depth — measured — with amounts and history hidden.

SPEED

Channels, shipped

TA's decisive present-tense advantage: multi-asset Lightning channels in production. If you need trustless instant settlement this quarter, TA wins — at the price of the other two columns. Ultraviolet answers with mempool-visible-in-seconds payments, kernel-native eltoo channels for relationships, and hash-locked gateway swaps into Lightning itself.

The RWA angle sharpens the quantum argument: tokenized treasuries and bonds are long-duration instruments. A 10-year note issued on classical rails in 2026 must remain unforgeable into the mid-2030s — the exact window CRQC estimates fight over. Payments can migrate when the threat lands; issued long-dated assets have to outlive it.

The verdict

Taproot Assets is what excellent engineering achieves within the classical UTXO-bound model; Ultraviolet is what becomes possible by leaving it. Full deep-dive →

Adjacent designs

SuperScalar & channel factories

The strongest classical answer to receiving-without-a-UTXO: 127 clients behind one MuSig2 taproot output, no covenants, no LSP trust — implemented and exercised on signet (July 2026), with a real on-chain penalty proving the "revealed-secret poison." Two contrasts: the factory's entire trust model rests on a single aggregated Schnorr point holding 128 parties' funds — factories concentrate quantum exposure where Ultraviolet's keyless records eliminate it. And the report's own safety-critical core ("the double-sign defense is where the whole safety rests" — each signer's persisted refusal to co-sign conflicts) is the equivocation problem Ultraviolet solves structurally: one deterministic nullifier, first on-chain occurrence wins, no signer state to lose. Its cheater-funds-the-penalty pattern is prior art for Blacklight's bonds-are-notes slashing.

Also in the field: Shielded CSV (Blockstream/Alpen/ZeroSync) contributed the architecture Ultraviolet builds on — 64-byte nullifiers, proof-carrying data, constant-size receive — but its nullifier check is a Schnorr verification, so it is not post-quantum. RGB v0.12 modernized its VM but removed confidential amounts, never shipped history compression, and remains UTXO-bound. Full analysis in the spec/10-COMPARISONS.
